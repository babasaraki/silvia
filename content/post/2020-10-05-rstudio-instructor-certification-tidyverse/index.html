Why did I get trained?

Teaching R was not something I thought I’d ever get into when I started using R for my postdoc position. I had taken programming courses as an undergraduate student and still found myself hiking up a learning curve. My learning came primarily from code chunks from coworkers, StackOverflow, and the as-needed browsing of R for Data Science. This approach was ok and served me to get where I needed to go for work projects, but it wasn’t methodical by any stretch of the imagination.

Meanwhile, CHOP R User group lunch-and-learns and R-Ladies Philly meet-ups were showing me the robustness and versatility of R across disciplines and it quickly became clear there were very tangible ways I could use these skills to support a community organizing collective that I’m a part of. This would require teaching R to folks that don’t program and have very little, if any, experience handling data. I didn’t feel prepared to take this on knowing I had hiked down learning paths that I wouldn’t necessarily recommend to others.

Fast-forward and I can now say I feel prepared because the RStudio Instructor Training provided all the tools I need in order to take the next steps with my collective!

Read on to find out what I learned before I decided to pursue the certification, what the certification experience was like for me, and resources to support you in your own journey if you decide to take this path.
